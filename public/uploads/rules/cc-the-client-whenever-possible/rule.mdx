CC the client from the beginning (unless it's obvious internal communication). This allows the client to see progress towards speaking with the right person and also gives them an opportunity to correct any details we might have got wrong.

<endIntro />

## The default - CC the client

When in doubt, CC the client. Transparency builds trust, avoids surprises, and gives the client a chance to flag anything that's wrong before it becomes a problem.

  to="Dave"
  cc="Bob Northwind"
  bcc=""
  subject="Northwind - Introducing Dave for the API integration work"
  shouldDisplayBody={true}
  body={<>

## Hi Dave

I'd like to introduce you to Bob from Northwind. Bob, Dave is our integration specialist and will be picking up the API work we discussed last week.

Dave, could you reach out to Bob to organise a kick-off call?

  </>}

## When you should **not** CC the client

There are 2 main exceptions.

### 1. Internal business, HR, or anything not directly to do with the client

If the email is about internal SSW matters - HR, internal resourcing, technical decisions that don't affect deliverables, admin, or finance - leave the client off. They don't need to see it, and including them adds noise.

Just letting you know I've put in a leave request for next Friday. Can you cover the stand-up?

### 2. Bookings - use your judgement

Bookings are the tricky case. There is **no hard and fast rule** - it comes down to judgement.

On one hand, you want the client to know who is booked for them and when. On the other hand:

* When a client has multiple people being booked at SSW, CC'ing them on every booking email fills up their inbox and calendar of bookings for the day
* Several clients have specifically told us they don't want that

A good way to decide:

* **Single or occasional booking** - CC the client so they know who is coming
* **Client with many ongoing bookings** - skip the CC, especially if they've told you they don't want them
* **Not sure?** - ask the client once how they'd like to be kept informed, then stick to that
