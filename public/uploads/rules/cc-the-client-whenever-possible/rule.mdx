CC the client from the beginning (unless it's obvious internal communication). This allows the client to see progress towards speaking with the right person and also gives them an opportunity to correct any details we might have got wrong.

<endIntro />

## When you **should** CC the client

Keeping the client in the loop builds trust and avoids surprises. CC the client when:

* **Short bookings (1 week or less)** - The client should see the scheduling emails so they know who is coming and when
* **Scope or requirement discussions** - Any conversation that could affect what gets delivered
* **Decisions that impact the project** - Budget changes, timeline shifts, or priority changes
* **Progress updates and status reports** - Keep the client informed on milestones
* **Escalations or blockers** - If something is going wrong, the client should know early
* **Initial introductions** - When connecting the client with a new team member or specialist

## When you **should not** CC the client

Not every internal email needs client visibility. Avoid CC'ing the client when:

* **Long bookings (more than 1 week)** - For ongoing engagements, the client doesn't need to see every scheduling email. They already know the team is working on their project
* **Internal technical discussions** - Code reviews, architecture debates, or tooling decisions
* **Internal HR or admin matters** - Leave requests, internal resourcing shuffles
* **Conversations that have moved to purely internal topics** - Once a thread shifts away from client-relevant content, [remove the client from the loop](/email-add-or-remove-someone-from-conversation)

### Why long bookings are different

When a developer is booked for a short engagement (e.g., a few days), the client benefits from seeing the booking email — it confirms who is working, when they start, and gives them a chance to flag any issues.

For long bookings (more than 1 week), the client is already aware of the ongoing engagement. CC'ing them on every internal scheduling email adds unnecessary noise and doesn't provide value. Instead, keep them informed through regular status updates and Sprint Reviews.
