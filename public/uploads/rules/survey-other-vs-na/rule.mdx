When designing survey questions, 'Other' and 'N/A' serve very different purposes. Mixing them up leads to messy data and makes it harder to draw meaningful conclusions.

<endIntro />

**'Other'** means "my answer exists but isn't listed." The respondent has a valid answer - you just didn't include it as a predefined option. It's common to pair this with a free-text field so they can tell you what their answer actually is.

**'N/A'** means "this question doesn't apply to me." The respondent isn't choosing a different answer - the entire question is irrelevant to them.

### Add specificity to N/A options

A plain 'N/A' tells you nothing about *why* the question doesn't apply. Adding context to N/A options gives you useful segmentation data for free.

::: greybox
**Survey question:** "How do you find the UX of our admin dashboard?"

- Great
- OK
- Bad
- N/A

:::
::: bad
Figure: Bad example - You don't know why it's N/A. Are they a dev who doesn't care about UX? Or someone who has never used the dashboard?
:::

::: greybox
**Survey question:** "How do you find the UX of our admin dashboard?"

- Great
- OK
- Bad
- N/A - I've never used the admin dashboard
- N/A - I'm a dev (I use the API instead)

:::
::: good
Figure: Good example - Specific N/A options tell you exactly why the question doesn't apply, giving you richer data without asking extra questions
:::
