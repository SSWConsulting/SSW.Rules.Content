When you need someone to act on something buried in a busy chat or channel, don't make them hunt for it. Copy the link to the exact message and paste it into your follow-up, so they are one click from where they need to act.

<endIntro />

In Microsoft Teams, hover over the message, click the **Meatball menu (...)**, and choose **Copy link**. Paste that link into a short, direct follow-up.

    RE: \[EMAIL SUBJECT]

    \[INSERT LINK TO MESSAGE]
  </>}
  figurePrefix="good"
  figure="Clear context, a numbered ask, and a direct link to the exact message that needs attention"
/>

## Why this works

* The reader does not waste time scrolling a channel to find what you're referring to
* The `RE: EMAIL SUBJECT` line gives instant context, so they know what it relates to before opening anything
* The numbered task makes the required action unambiguous
* A permalink survives even when the channel keeps moving, so the message is still findable later
