When you need someone to act on something buried in a busy chat or channel, don't make them hunt for it. Copy the link to the exact message and paste it into your follow-up, so they are one click from where they need to act.

<endIntro />

In Microsoft Teams, hover over the message, click the `Meatball menu (...)`, and then choose `Copy link`. Paste that link into a short follow-up message.

<boxEmbed
  body={<>
    Hi Adam,
    
    Can you check chat and \[ACTION] when you get a chance?
  </>}
  figurePrefix="bad"
  figure="No context and no link, so the reader has to scroll the channel to find the right message"
/>

<boxEmbed
  body={<>
    Hi Adam,
    
    RE: \[SUBJECT]
    
    1\. Please see chat and \[ACTION]:
    
    \[INSERT LINK TO MESSAGE]
  </>}
  figurePrefix="good"
  figure="Clear context, a numbered ask, and a direct link to the exact message that needs the vote"
/>

***

## Why this works

* The reader does not waste time scrolling a channel to find what you're referring to
* The `RE: [SUBJECT]` line gives instant context, so they know what it relates to before opening anything
* The numbered task makes the required action unambiguous
* A permalink survives even when the channel keeps moving, so the message is still findable later
