When you need someone to act on something buried in a busy chat or channel, don't make them hunt for it. Copy the link to the exact message and paste it into your follow-up, so they are one click from where they need to act.

<endIntro />

In Microsoft Teams, hover over the message, click the **Meatball menu (...)**, and choose **Copy link**. Paste that link into a short, direct follow-up.

## Examples

<boxEmbed
  style="greybox"
  body={<>
    Hey team, can everyone jump into the channel and vote on the Tina options when you get a chance?
  </>}
  figurePrefix="bad"
  figure="Bad example - No context and no link, so the reader has to scroll the channel to find the right message"
/>

<boxEmbed
  style="greybox"
  body={<>
    Hey Adam, quick one<br /><br />
    RE: LinkedIn - Tina skills legacy<br /><br />
    1. Please see chat and make your vote: <u>Alex Blum [SSW]: Everyone Please vote on the following options:</u> (linked directly to the message)
  </>}
  figurePrefix="good"
  figure="Good example - Clear context, a numbered ask, and a direct link to the exact message that needs the vote"
/>

## Why this works

* The reader does not waste time scrolling a channel to find what you mean
* The `RE:` line gives instant context, so they know what it relates to before opening anything
* The numbered ask makes the required action unambiguous
* A permalink survives even when the channel keeps moving, so the message is still findable later
