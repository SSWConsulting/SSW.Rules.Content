Recording a public video is easier when you do not try to get it perfect alone.

Good coaching helps you improve the message, delivery, and final edit before too much time is spent recording the wrong thing.

<endIntro />

## The problem

Many people record a full video first, then ask for feedback after.

That often means the issues are found too late:

* The content is unclear
* The delivery feels unnatural
* The presenter talks for too long
* The best points are buried
* The editor has to guess what to cut

## The solution

When recording an important video, use these 3 coaching tips.

### 1. Get a coach

Do not record alone if the content matters.

Get someone technical, or someone who understands the topic, to watch and coach you. They can help you check whether the content is correct, clear, and useful.

This is especially important before recording public videos.

See rule: Do you get your content approved before recording a public video?

✅ Figure: Good example - A technical coach reviews the message before and during the recording

2\. Get interrupted

For your first version, do not wait until the end to get feedback.

Ask your coach to interrupt you as you go. This helps you improve the video while the ideas are fresh.

They can stop you when:

The message is unclear

You are using too many words

The intro is too slow

A better example is needed

Something is technically wrong

This feels less natural at first, but it saves time and makes the final video much stronger.

✅ Figure: Good example - The coach interrupts during the first take so the presenter can fix problems immediately

3\. Clap when you make a mistake

When you make a mistake, clap.

This creates a clear spike in the audio, which helps the editor quickly find the mistake later.

After clapping, pause briefly, then restart the sentence or section.

This simple habit makes editing much easier and avoids wasting time searching through the footage.

✅ Figure: Good example - The presenter claps after a mistake so the editor can easily find the cut point

Tip: Do not edit it all yourself

If you are not a video editor, do not spend hours trying to perfect the edit yourself.

Instead:

Get the raw recordings ready

Sit with a TV person

Step through the footage together

Decide what to cut, simplify, or re-record

This is usually faster and gives a better result.

The 3 coaching tips

Get a coach – Ask someone technical to check the message and delivery

Get interrupted – Get feedback as you go, especially on the first version

Clap when you make a mistake – Give editors a clear reference point
