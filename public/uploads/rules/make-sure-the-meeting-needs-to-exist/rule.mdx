Meetings are great for brainstorming ideas, getting in sync, and making decisions together. For the right topics, they can be much more effective than a long group chat thread.

However, meetings are expensive. A 1-hour meeting with 5 people is not a 1-hour cost. It is at least 5 hours, plus the time it takes everyone to switch context and get back into the zone afterwards.

<endIntro />

A good meeting has [an agenda](https://www.ssw.com.au/rules/share-the-agenda) and helps the team:

* Understand who needs to be involved
* Update shared knowledge
* Clarify the group’s goal
* See how each person’s work contributes to the outcome
* Discuss topics where back-and-forth is needed
* Align on a decision and shared commitment

***

## Meeting or something else?

Use a meeting when the topic needs live discussion, fast back-and-forth, brainstorming, negotiation, or shared commitment.

Before booking a meeting, [consider a few factors](https://www.ssw.com.au/rules/when-to-email-chat-call-or-meet) to decide whether another option would work better:

### Email

Use email when the message is formal, needs a clear record, or does not require an immediate response.

<imageEmbed alt="Image" size="small" showBorder={true} figurePrefix="none" figure="" src="/uploads/rules/make-sure-the-meeting-needs-to-exist/meeting-could-be-email.jpg" />

### Group chat

Use group chat when the topic is quick, informal, and only needs a short answer.

### Comments on documents

Use document comments when feedback is needed on specific wording, [designs](https://www.ssw.com.au/rules/hand-over-mockups-to-developers#get-feedback-for-your-design-work), specs, or decisions.

### Recorded video or voice note

Use a recorded video or voice note when you need to explain context, show a screen, or walk through something without interrupting people.

### Ticket, issue, or task

Use a ticket, issue, or task when the topic needs ownership, tracking, and follow-up.
