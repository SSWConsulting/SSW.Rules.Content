In the course of your employment, you may produce a number of videos for a project, a client, and/or for your profile.

<endIntro />

## 🌍 Public company/product YouTube channel

Use the company/product/regional YouTube channel for best practices content.

* **Product-related content** (e.g. [YakShaver](https://www.youtube.com/@SSWYakShaver)) - Upload to the proper product channel, collaborate with the company channel (e.g. [SSW TV](https://www.youtube.com/@SSWTV))
* **Office-specific content** (e.g. office walkthroughs, retreats) - Collaborate with the regional channel (e.g. [SSWEurope](https://www.youtube.com/@SSWEurope))

<imageEmbed alt="Image" size="large" showBorder={true} figurePrefix="good" figure="Collaboration between YouTube channels: SSW TV and SSW TimePro" src="/uploads/rules/where-to-upload-work-related-videos/video-example-collaboration-youtube-channels.png" />

Learn more on [inviting collaborators to your video](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/16554898?sjid=10366312048070105784-NC).

## 🔗 Unlisted company/product YouTube channel

For [Done Videos](/record-a-quick-and-dirty-done-video) or content not intended for general audiences, upload them to the product or company YouTube channel as **unlisted**.

<boxEmbed
  body={<>
    ### Transitory Junk
    
    You might think about creating two channels:
    
    * TimePro – for product features and announcements
    * TimeProInsiders – for Sprint Reviews and other short-lived updates
    
    Technically, that works. Strategically, it doesn’t.
    
    Keep everything in a single channel because:
    
    * Activity builds trust – An active channel signals momentum and progress. If only polished feature posts are published, it can look quieter than it really is
    * One place to subscribe – We don’t want people choosing between channels or missing updates because they followed the wrong one
    
    Yes, some content is transitory. That’s OK.
    
    Transparency and visibility are more valuable than over-curating.
  </>}

## 🏢 Client YouTube channel

For important clients, upload the videos under an extra channel for that client project.

## 👤 Personal YouTube channel

In rare cases, for trivial content, you may put them on **your own YouTube channel** under a [dedicated playlist](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/57792) called "**Work - &#123;&#123; COMPANY NAME &#125;&#125;**".

You can then look back to them a decade later and see how much you have improved fondly (e.g. [Adam Cogan | Work](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLHfpOqYBdyCipla9YEHKcFyzarM9RbFLx))
