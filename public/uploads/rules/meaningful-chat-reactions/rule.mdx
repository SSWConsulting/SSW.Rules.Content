Clear, **written text** is by far the best way to communicate, so always try to write down your thoughts instead of relying on emojis reactions, which can easily be misinterpreted.

For example, in Teams chats, a 👍 (thumbs up) is often used to either **acknowledge a message** OR to **indicate that a task is done**. This can lead to ambiguity for the sender, especially when the task isn’t completed immediately.

## Keep reactions simple and meaningful

Emoji reactions are effective when they’re applied **consistently** and with **clear meaning**. They are a useful way to give quick responses and visually communicate the status or progress of a message at a glance.

<imageEmbed alt="Image" size="medium" showBorder={true} figurePrefix="none" figure="3 emojis you might use when recieve a message" src="/uploads/rules/meaningful-chat-reactions/3-emojis-you-might-use-when-recieve-a-message.png" />

To improve clarity and keep everyone aligned:

* React with 👀 (eyes) when you've seen the message and are working on it. Should be interpreted as *“I’m on it now… standby”*
* Once the task is complete, update your reaction to ✅ (green check) to signal it’s done

This simple habit reduces confusion, keeps chats clean, and ensures the whole team can track progress at a glance.
