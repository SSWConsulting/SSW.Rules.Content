Exceptional developers get promoted. A business recognises their talent and, rightly or wrongly, want that developer to lead others in the hope that they will teach and mentor the more junior team members to reach the same level of exceptionalism.

But being a great developer doesn't intrinsically make someone a great leader. It's a different set of skills, and can seem counterintuitive to a “High IC” (individual contributor).

How do you move from a hero IC engineer to a force multiplier of an entire team?

<endIntro />

<youtubeEmbed url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nStD2YFKSq8" description="Video: From Solo Superstar to Servant Leader | Empowering Engineering Teams (10 min)" />

Good leadership in the context of leading a development team is less about authority and more about creating the conditions where engineers can do their best work consistently. It blends technical credibility, people leadership, and systems thinking.
