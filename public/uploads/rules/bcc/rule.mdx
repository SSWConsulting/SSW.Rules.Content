The Bcc field allows you to send an email to recipients without revealing their addresses to others. It is commonly used to protect privacy and limit unnecessary replies.

<endIntro />

When you send bulk emails, use the Bcc field instead of the To field.

  to="alex@aaa.com, @brandon@bbb.com, charlie@ccc.com, debora@ddd.com"

  bcc=""

You can also use Bcc to avoid unwanted “Reply All” responses. Be transparent and state the reason clearly at the top of the email.

<emailEmbed
  from=""
  to=""
  cc=""
  bcc="SSWAll"
  subject="🧠⚡ Brisbane Brainstorming 2026 - Retro - Chewing the Fat 📈 "
  shouldDisplayBody={true}
  body={<>
    (BCCing SSWAll to avoid any accidental Reply Alls)
    
    ## Hey SSW'ers,
    
    &#123;&#123; EMAIL CONTENT &#125;&#125;
  </>}
  figurePrefix="good"
  figure="When recipients are Bcc’d and the reason is clearly explained at the top of the email"
/>
