Use two monitors when your work regularly involves cross-referencing information side by side. Keep your main task on one screen and supporting material on the other. This reduces window switching and makes it easier to compare, check, and continue working without losing context.

<endIntro />

This is especially useful for developers, analysts, and reviewers. Good examples are: 

* Code & Documentation
* Spreadsheet & Report
* Ticket & Logs 
* Source Material & Data Entry. 

The benefit is strongest when you need two active windows at once.

Do not oversell it. Two monitors are not a guaranteed big productivity boost for every task. They help most with comparison-heavy work. For more linear work, the gain can be much smaller.

Set them up properly. Put your primary monitor directly in front of you. Put the secondary monitor to the side. If you use both equally, center the pair together. A poor setup increases neck rotation and strain.

* Use two monitors if your job regularly needs two windows open at once.
* If you mostly work in one app at a time, one good monitor is enough, or possibly  better.
