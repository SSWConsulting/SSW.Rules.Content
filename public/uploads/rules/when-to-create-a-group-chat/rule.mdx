Create a group chat when the conversation involves multiple people, when you need fast input from a few people, or when the outcome should be visible to everyone involved. 

 

Group chats are useful for keeping context shared, avoiding side conversations, crowdsourcing opinions, and getting quick test passes or feedback without creating a meeting. See: [ Do you manage up?  ](https://www.ssw.com.au/rules/do-you-manage-up)


This approach is useful before starting a Teams call and you can simply say *"I'll call you in 5 mins"* in your group chat to let everyone in the group know that you need to chat with them.

Group chats should also be used to [replace meeting chats if they need non-attendees to join](/teams-meetings-vs-group-chats).

## What if people are sitting next to each other

Always add everybody into the chat, even if they’re sitting next to each other. If during the call messages are sent, everybody gets them.

## Make sure people are available

You shouldn't make the call if the participants have [Teams status](/use-the-status-message-in-teams) "In a call" or their status message suggests they shouldn't be disturbed.
