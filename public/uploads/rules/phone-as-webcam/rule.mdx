Most laptop webcams deliver mediocre video quality with poor low-light performance and limited dynamic range. When you're traveling for conferences or remote work, looking professional on video calls matters, but carrying dedicated camera gear is impractical.

Your phone already has a superior camera system with features like portrait mode, studio lighting, and better sensors. Modern operating systems make it easy to use your phone as a webcam with minimal setup.

<endIntro />

## Native solutions

### For iPhone + Mac users

**Apple Continuity Camera** is built directly into macOS Ventura (13+) and iOS 16+. Simply place your iPhone (XR or later) near your Mac and it automatically appears as a camera option in any video app.

**Features:**

* Center Stage - Auto-framing that keeps you in shot
* Portrait mode - Professional background blur
* Studio Light - Enhanced facial lighting
* Desk View - Shows your desk from above using the ultra-wide camera
* Works wirelessly or via USB cable

### For Android + Windows users

**Windows 11 Phone Link** enables Android phones (Android 10+) to function as wireless webcams without installing third-party apps.

**Setup:**

1. Go to Settings > Bluetooth & devices > Mobile devices
2. Connect your Android phone via Link to Windows
3. Enable camera sharing
4. Your phone camera appears in Teams, Zoom, or any video app

## Cross-platform apps

If you need more flexibility or are using different OS combinations:

* **Camo** - Professional quality with 1080p support (iPhone and Android)
* **DroidCam** - Free option for Android and iPhone (480p free, HD with DroidCamX)
* **EpocCam** - iOS-focused with easy setup for Mac and Windows

## Travel setup tips

When traveling, a simple setup can deliver professional results:

* Small tripod - Positions the camera at eye level
* Tablet as second monitor - Improves productivity
* Built-in laptop mic - Fine for casual calls
* DJI Mic Mini - Compact wireless mic for recordings and interviews (48-hour battery, 400m range, automatic noise cancellation)

Using gear you already have, plus small improvements to framing and audio, helps you look and sound professional whether you're presenting at a conference or joining a daily standup.
