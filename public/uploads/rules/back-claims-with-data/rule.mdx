Vague marketing claims like "much faster" or "industry-leading" tell the reader nothing they can verify. They read as opinion, not fact, so people discount them and AI answer engines skip right past them.

<endIntro />

## Replace qualitative claims with numbers

Whenever you write a claim about performance, cost, scale, or results, ask "what is the number?" If you cannot answer, the claim is not ready to publish.

Swap subjective adjectives for measured facts:

* "much faster" becomes "renders in 200ms — roughly 3× faster"
* "trusted by thousands" becomes "used by 4,200 teams"
* "saves you money" becomes "cuts hosting costs by 38%"

## Pair every statistic with its source

A number is only as trustworthy as its evidence. Link each statistic to the benchmark, study, or dataset it came from so readers and AI search tools can confirm it and cite it with confidence. For your own independent statistics, make available details on the methodology used and how to reproduce it.
