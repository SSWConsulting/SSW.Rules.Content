Misunderstanding due to miscommunication can cause major disasters at work or in life (see: [Tenerife airport disaster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tenerife_airport_disaster)).

<imageEmbed
  alt="Image"
  size="large"
  showBorder={false}
  captionStyle="badExample"
  captionText={"KLM Flight 4805 collides with Pan Am Flight 1736 at Tenerife Los Rodeos Airport"}
  src="/uploads/rules/do-you-repeat-back-the-specifics-of-a-request/flight-accident-1977.jpg"
/>


A good communication strategy when receiving instructions is to repeat back what's being said to avoid misunderstanding.

<endOfIntro />

Question:


<boxEmbed
  style="greybox"
  body={<>
    
1. Please add a checkbox on the right, and make it red.
  </>}
  captionStyle="default"
  captionText={""}
/>


Possible answers:


<boxEmbed
  style="greybox"
  body={<>
    "OK" or "Roger That"
  </>}
  captionStyle="badExample"
  captionText={"Bad Example"}
/>



<boxEmbed
  style="greybox"
  body={<>
    OK, I will add a red checkbox on the right
  </>}
  captionStyle="goodExample"
  captionText={"Good Example - repeat back the task"}
/>
