[Sitefinity](https://www.progress.com/sitefinity-cms) is a strong choice for enterprise websites that need structured content, governance, personalization, and marketing tools built into the platform.

It is designed more for large organizations than for small marketing sites or simple blogs.

<endIntro />

## Use Sitefinity when

* You need strong permissions, workflows, and publishing governance
* Your business requires multilingual content and enterprise-level scalability
* Personalization, segmentation, and marketing automation are important
* Your team works in a Microsoft and .NET environment
* You need both content management and digital marketing capabilities in one platform

## Consider another CMS when

* Your project is a simple marketing website or blog
* Your team wants a lightweight and lower-cost CMS
* You need a fully headless API-first platform like [Contentful](https://www.contentful.com) or [Sanity](https://www.sanity.io)
* Your website is primarily Markdown-driven and a Git-based workflow would be better suited with [TinaCMS](https://tina.io)
* A faster setup with less enterprise overhead would be better served by [WordPress](https://wordpress.org) or [Umbraco](https://umbraco.com)

## Why this matters

Choosing Sitefinity makes sense when governance and enterprise features are part of the business need.

For smaller projects, it can introduce unnecessary complexity, cost, and maintenance.
