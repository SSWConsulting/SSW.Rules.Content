Anonymous content is a trust problem. When a page has no named author, no bio, and no dates, readers cannot tell who wrote it or whether they know the subject, and neither can the systems that rank and cite content.

Google's quality systems evaluate content through the lens of **E-E-A-T** (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trust). Its guidance on [creating helpful, reliable, people-first content](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/creating-helpful-content) asks "Is this content written or reviewed by an expert or enthusiast who demonstrably knows the topic well?" AI answer engines like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews do the same: they favour and cite content from identifiable, credible authors, and they trust and quote anonymous content far less.

Showing clear authorship is one of the cheapest, highest-impact trust signals you can add. It helps you rank in classic search and makes you far more likely to be named as a source when an AI answers a question.

<endIntro />

## What E-E-A-T stands for

* **Experience** - the author has first-hand, real-world experience with the topic
* **Expertise** - the author has genuine knowledge or qualifications in the field
* **Authoritativeness** - the author and site are recognised as a go-to source
* **Trust** - the content is accurate, transparent, and honestly sourced

Trust is the centre of E-E-A-T. The other three signals exist to establish it — as Google [puts it](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/creating-helpful-content), "trust is most important. The others contribute to trust."

## Show a real author byline

Every substantial article should carry a visible byline naming a real person, not "Admin" or the company name alone. The byline should state the author's role or credential and link to a dedicated author bio page.

## Show publish and last-updated dates

Display a clear published date and a visible last-updated date. Freshness is a ranking and citation factor, and dates let readers and AI engines judge whether the information is current.

## Be transparent about sourcing

Link to the primary sources behind your claims. Transparent sourcing demonstrates Trust and lets AI engines verify your content against the references you cite, making it safer for them to quote you.
